A tribute to a courageous woman and tireless advocate for peace and equality:

Remembering Rosaline Baatuolkuu Obeng-Ofori

A Trail-blazing member of the pioneering generation of post-independence African literature giants, Florence Onyebuchi “Buchi” Emecheta, died in London on January 25 at the age of 72 and the word is the poorer for it. Born of Igbo parents from Nigeria’s oil-rich Delta region in July 1944, her father was a railway worker who died when she was nine. She was brought up by her mother in the cosmopolitan colonial capital of Lagos, having to convince her parents to let her join her brother to go to school. She won a scholarship to Methodist Girls’ High School at 10, but left school seven years later, marrying Sylvester Onwordi to whom she had been engaged since she was 11.
